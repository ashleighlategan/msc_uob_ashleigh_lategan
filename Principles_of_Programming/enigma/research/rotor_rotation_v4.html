Enigma rotor rotation — two coordinate systems

Position: A Input: A (row 0) Wire exits drum: B Rotor II sees: B
Frame 1 — the drum
The wiring lives here. Pin B always connects to contact D — soldered inside, never changes. When you rotate, this whole frame moves.
Frame 2 — the housing
The outside world. The next rotor speaks this language. A is always A, fixed in space. The drum rotates inside it.
Step 1 — enter drum
(input + offset) % 26
Translate from housing into the drum's frame. Housing row A lines up with drum row offset.
Step 2 — read wiring
wiring[pin_index]
Purely inside the drum. No offset here — just follow the wire.
Step 3 — exit drum
(wire_out - offset) % 26
Translate back to housing. Subtract the same offset to get what Rotor II actually sees.
+offset and −offset are not two separate corrections. They are the same rotation seen from opposite ends of the drum — you shift in to enter the drum's world, and shift back out to return to the housing's world.
