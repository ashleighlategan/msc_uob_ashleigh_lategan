Enigma rotor position trace

Input A through rotors III → II → I → Reflector B → return. Three scenarios varying Rotor III's effective position after rotation.

Rotation always happens before the signal
On a real Enigma machine — and on the online emulator — the rightmost rotor physically advances before the electrical signal passes through. This means the position you set is never the position the signal actually sees.

That is why setting the emulator to AAA and pressing A gives B, not U — the rotor advances to B first, then the signal travels through. This matches Scenario 2 below, not Scenario 1.

In your code: always rotate first, then encode.
Step 1 — rotate

Rotor III advances by 1

e.g. A → B, or Z → A
Step 2 — check notch

Did III hit its notch? If so, II advances too

(double-step also applies)
Step 3 — encode

Signal passes through the rotors at their new positions

This is what the scenarios below show

The hardest part — two coordinate systems

This is the bit that trips everyone up. Once you see it, it clicks permanently.

Frame 1 — the drum
The wiring lives here. Pin B always connects to contact D — soldered inside, never changes. When you rotate, this whole frame moves.
Frame 2 — the housing
The outside world. The next rotor speaks this language. A is always A, fixed in space. The drum rotates inside it.
Step 1 — enter drum
(input + offset) % 26
Translate from housing into the drum's frame. Housing row A lines up with drum row offset.
Step 2 — read wiring
wiring[pin_index]
Purely inside the drum. No offset here — just follow the wire.
Step 3 — exit drum
(wire_out - offset) % 26
Translate back to housing. Subtract the same offset to get what the next rotor actually sees.
+offset and −offset are not two separate corrections. They are the same rotation seen from opposite ends of the drum — you shift in to enter the drum's world, and shift back out to return to the housing's world. The same logic applies on the return pass: +offset to enter, reverse lookup, −offset to exit.
Worked example — Rotor III at position C (offset 2), input A
InputA = index 0  (housing frame)
+ offset(0 + 2) % 26 = 2 → pin C  (drum frame)
wiring[2]= F  (drum frame)
− offset(5 − 2) % 26 = 3 → D  (housing frame) — Rotor II sees D
